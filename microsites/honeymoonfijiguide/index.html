Why Fiji for Your Honeymoon?

Fiji is the most popular honeymoon destination for Australians and New Zealanders — and for good reason. It's close enough to be accessible (3.5 hours from Sydney, 3 hours from Auckland) but remote enough to feel genuinely far away. The combination of extraordinary natural beauty, warm hospitality, and world-class resort infrastructure is difficult to match anywhere at this price point.

The Fijian concept of love and celebration is woven into the culture. Staff at Fijian resorts genuinely celebrate honeymooners — you'll be greeted with flowers, petals on the bed, and a warmth that doesn't feel performative. It's one of those destinations that lives up to its reputation.

Best kept secret: Always tell your resort, airline, and transfer provider that you're on your honeymoon. Upgrades, complimentary extras, and preferential treatment are common — but only if they know to give them to you.

Planning Your Fiji Honeymoon

Best Time to Visit

The dry season (May–October) is ideal for honeymooners. June and July are particularly good — days are warm and sunny, humidity is lower, evenings are cool, and the water is crystal clear. November–April brings tropical rains and higher humidity, though prices are lower and resorts less crowded.

How Long to Stay

  • 7 nights: Minimum to genuinely unwind. One island, one resort. Enough time to do nothing properly.
  • 10–14 nights: Allows combination of a remote island resort with 2–3 nights on Denarau for island day trips and dining variety.
  • Combination itinerary: 4 nights Denarau → 7 nights private island → 2 nights Denarau on return. Popular Australian pattern.

Getting to Your Resort

Your honeymoon experience begins before you arrive at the resort — don't let airport logistics be the first memory. A private transfer from Nadi Airport to Port Denarau Marina (or your resort directly, for Denarau properties) sets the tone.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is Fiji good for a honeymoon?
Yes — Fiji is one of the world's genuinely great honeymoon destinations. Close to Australia and New Zealand, extraordinarily beautiful, with resort infrastructure built for couples, and Fijian hospitality that makes every guest feel celebrated. It consistently ranks among the top honeymoon destinations for couples from the Southern Hemisphere.
Does Fiji have overwater bungalows?
Yes. Fiji's most famous overwater bure property is Likuliku Lagoon Resort in the Mamanuca Islands. The overwater bures sit directly over the lagoon with glass floors and private ocean access. Several other Fiji resorts now offer semi-overwater or lagoon-facing premium bure options.
How much does a Fiji honeymoon cost in Australian dollars?
A 7-night mid-range honeymoon at a Mamanuca or Denarau resort typically costs AUD $5,000–9,000 per couple including flights from Sydney. Premium private island resorts run AUD $12,000–25,000 for 7 nights. Ultra-luxury properties like Turtle Island or Laucala Island are AUD $30,000+ for 7 nights all-inclusive.
When is the best time for a Fiji honeymoon?
June to August is the sweet spot — dry season, warm days, cooler evenings, and exceptional water clarity for snorkelling. May and September–October are also excellent with slightly lower prices. Avoid January–March (wet season peak) unless you're on a tight budget and flexible about weather.
What activities are best for honeymooners in Fiji?
Sunset sailing cruises, private beach picnics (arranged by your resort), couples spa treatments, snorkelling the outer reef at sunset, kava ceremonies in a traditional village, and evening bonfires on the beach. The activities themselves matter less than the pace — Fiji's greatest gift to honeymooners is permission to slow down completely.
