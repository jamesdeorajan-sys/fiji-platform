Everything You Need to Know About Horse Riding in Fiji

Horse riding in Fiji is one of those travel experiences that stays with you forever. The image of riding a calm, sure-footed horse along Natadola Beach — white sand underfoot, turquoise water beside you, the mountains of Viti Levu behind — is genuinely one of the most beautiful things you can do in the South Pacific.

Fiji's horse riding tours have grown significantly since 2020. What was once a niche activity is now one of the most searched and booked experiences for visitors arriving in Nadi. This guide gives you the honest picture — what it actually costs, what to expect, which tour is right for your group, and how to book without losing 25% of your money to Viator or GetYourGuide.

Natadola Beach — Fiji's Signature Horse Riding Location

Natadola Beach is widely considered one of Fiji's most beautiful beaches. Located about 50km south of Nadi on the Queens Highway, it's a 2km arc of white coral sand with calm, shallow water — ideal for swimming, and even better for riding horses along the shoreline.

The standard Natadola Beach ride runs for 50 minutes and costs AU$95 per person. Hotel pickup from Nadi and Denarau is included on most packages. The horses are calm, experienced beach horses — completely suitable for first-time riders. No experience is required and the guides are experienced, friendly, and patient with all riding abilities.

If you want more time in the saddle, the Combinational Horse Riding tour extends to 2.5 hours and currently runs at AU$100 (60% off the standard AU$250 rate) — exceptional value for what is genuinely a highlight-of-Fiji experience.

Coral Coast Horse Riding — The Adventure Alternative

The Coral Coast horse riding tours depart from the Sigatoka area on Fiji's scenic Coral Coast, about 90 minutes from Nadi. These tours (2.5 hours, from AU$180) offer more varied terrain — you'll ride along the beach, through river crossings, and into the edge of the rainforest. The Waterfall and Rainforest Riding tour adds a waterfall stop, making it one of the more complete adventure experiences in Fiji.

Choose Natadola for the classic Fiji beach experience. Choose Coral Coast if your group wants something more active and varied. Both are outstanding.

Why Book Direct Instead of Viator or GetYourGuide

Viator and GetYourGuide charge tour operators between 20-30% commission on every booking. That cost is built into the price you pay — or worse, the operator receives less income and can invest less in their horses, guides, and equipment. When you book direct through tourfijitours.com or fijitourtransfers.com, 100% of your payment goes directly to the local Fiji operator. Same tour. Same horses. No middleman.

What to Expect on the Day

Your driver will collect you from your Nadi or Denarau hotel, usually in the morning (early starts beat the midday heat). The drive to Natadola takes 45-60 minutes — your guide will brief you on the route as you travel. At the beach, you'll be matched with a horse, receive a brief orientation, and then you're off. There's no need to have ridden before — the horses know what they're doing even if you don't.

  • Wear comfortable clothing — shorts or light pants, closed-toe shoes if possible
  • Bring sunscreen and a hat — the Fiji sun is strong year-round
  • Cameras and phones are welcome but hold them securely while riding
  • Helmets are available and recommended for younger riders
  • Horses are calm and well-trained — but follow your guide's instructions at all times

Frequently Asked Questions

Horse Riding in Fiji — Common Questions

How much does horse riding in Fiji cost?
Horse riding in Fiji starts from AU$95 for a 50-minute ride at Natadola Beach — Fiji's most beautiful beach. The Combinational tour (2.5 hours) is currently available from AU$100 (60% off). Coral Coast rides start from AU$180 for 2.5 hours including varied beach, river and rainforest terrain. Book direct at tourfijitours.com for the best rates.
Where is the best horse riding in Fiji?
The top two locations are Natadola Beach (Fiji's most iconic white sand beach, 50km south of Nadi) and the Coral Coast near Sigatoka. Natadola is perfect for a classic Fiji beach ride. Coral Coast offers longer rides with more varied terrain including waterfall stops and rainforest. Both are outstanding experiences.
Do I need experience for Fiji horse riding tours?
No experience required. All Fiji horse riding tours are suitable for complete beginners. Guides provide full instruction before the ride begins. The horses are calm, trained beach horses — children and adults of all ages and abilities ride comfortably.
Is hotel pickup included?
Yes, hotel pickup and drop-off from Nadi, Denarau, and most Coral Coast hotels is included on most horse riding tours. Confirm your hotel at booking. Tours typically depart in the morning to avoid the midday heat.
What is the best time of year for horse riding in Fiji?
Horse riding runs year-round. The dry season (May to October) offers the most comfortable conditions with lower humidity. December to April is wetter but tours still operate and the landscape is lush and dramatic. Natadola Beach rides are beautiful in any weather.
How do I book horse riding in Fiji without paying Viator commission?
Book directly at tourfijitours.com or fijitourtransfers.com — both are commission-free. Pay via Square (all credit cards accepted). Or WhatsApp +61 478 886 145 for instant confirmation. You save the 20-30% Viator adds to every booking.
Is Natadola Beach horse riding better than Coral Coast?
Both are excellent but different. Natadola (50 min, AU$95) is the classic Fiji experience — pure white sand, calm ocean, Fiji at its most beautiful. Coral Coast (2.5 hrs, AU$180) is more adventurous with river crossings, rainforest, and waterfall stops. Choose Natadola for ease and beauty; Coral Coast for adventure and variety.
What should I wear for horse riding in Fiji?
Comfortable clothing — shorts or light pants, closed-toe shoes if possible (thongs are usually fine). Strong sunscreen and a hat are essential in the Fiji sun. Secure your camera and phone while riding. Helmets are available and recommended for younger riders.
