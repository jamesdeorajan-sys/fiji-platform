Getting from Nadi Airport — what you need to know first

Nadi International Airport (NAN) is Fiji's main international gateway. The moment you clear customs, you'll face a choice: pre-booked transfer, taxi, or carrier bus. Here's the honest local's view on each.

There is no Uber, no Grab, no Ola in Fiji. The safest, most predictable option for families and first-time visitors is a pre-booked airport transfer. Your driver meets you in arrivals holding your name board. Your flight is tracked. If you're delayed, they wait.

Pre-booked transfer (recommended)
Fixed price. Flight tracked. Driver in arrivals with name board. Free supermarket stop. Best for families, first visits, late-night arrivals.
Local taxi
Available outside arrivals. Always negotiate the price before you get in. Nadi Airport to Denarau: approx FJD $20–30. No meter, no guarantees.
Carrier bus
Very cheap — FJD $2–5. But infrequent, crowded, no luggage space, and stops everywhere. Not recommended with bags or after a long flight.

Getting to Port Denarau for island ferries

If you're heading to the Yasawa Islands or Mamanuca Islands — Blue Lagoon Cruises, Yasawa Flyer, or South Sea Cruises — you depart from Port Denarau Marina. A transfer from Nadi Airport to Port Denarau Marina costs FJD $55 sedan / FJD $79 minivan / FJD $110 minibus.

Allow at least 30–45 minutes before your ferry departure. Ferries do not wait. If your flight is delayed, WhatsApp Nadi Airport Transfers immediately — they monitor flight arrivals and can advise on options.

Frequently asked questions

These are the exact questions Fiji visitors have asked Lagi — answered directly.

  • How do I get from Nadi Airport to my resort? +
    Book a pre-arranged transfer at nadiairporttransfers.com before you fly. Your driver meets you in arrivals with a name board. The booking form asks for your hotel name, arrival date, flight number, and number of passengers. You receive instant email confirmation. Flight delays are automatically tracked — your driver adjusts.
  • Does Fiji have Uber or taxis? +
    No Uber or rideshare apps operate in Fiji. Local taxis are available outside arrivals but prices are negotiated — always agree the fare before getting in. Nadi Airport to Denarau by taxi is approximately FJD $20–30 but this varies. Pre-booked transfers are more reliable, especially for families and night arrivals.
  • How much is a transfer from Nadi Airport to the Hilton Denarau? +
    A transfer from Nadi Airport to Hilton Fiji Beach Resort on Denarau Island costs FJD $49 by sedan (up to 3 passengers), FJD $69 by minivan (up to 7), or FJD $99 by minibus (up to 12). The journey takes approximately 15–20 minutes. The same fare applies to Sheraton, Westin, Sofitel, and Radisson on Denarau.
  • How do I get from Nadi Airport to Port Denarau for the ferry? +
    A transfer from Nadi Airport to Port Denarau Marina costs FJD $55 sedan / FJD $79 minivan / FJD $110 minibus. Port Denarau is where Blue Lagoon Cruises, Yasawa Flyer, and South Sea Cruises depart from. Allow 30–45 minutes before your ferry. Ferries do not hold for late arrivals. Book your transfer and ferry together for peace of mind.
  • What is the best way to get from Nadi to Suva without a hire car? +
    Three options: (1) Private transfer — most comfortable, FJD $319 sedan, 3.5–4 hours, door to door. (2) Express bus — Fiji Bus Express runs Nadi to Suva. Inexpensive but slow and no luggage guarantee. (3) Carrier (local) bus — very cheap but takes 5+ hours with many stops. For most visitors, a private transfer is worth it for the time saved.
  • Can I make a supermarket stop during my transfer? +
    Yes — Nadi Airport Transfers includes a free supermarket stop on the way to your resort. Request it when booking or tell your driver on arrival. Popular for families who want to stock up on drinks, snacks, baby supplies, sunscreen, and groceries before reaching their accommodation.
  • Is there a night surcharge for airport transfers in Fiji? +
    Yes — a 20% night surcharge applies to transfers between 10pm and 6am. Many flights from Australia and New Zealand arrive in Fiji late at night or early morning. Factor this in when budgeting. The surcharge is applied to the base one-way fare. A Denarau sedan at FJD $49 becomes approximately FJD $59 on a night transfer.
  • We have a rental car — can we drive ourselves from the airport? +
    Yes — driving in Fiji is on the left side of the road. Roads from Nadi Airport to Denarau and the Coral Coast are generally in good condition. The Queens Road south to the Coral Coast is well sealed. For remote areas or the Highlands, a 4WD is recommended. Rental cars are available at Nadi Airport from Budget, Avis, and local operators.
